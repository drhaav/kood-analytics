Methodology
Cross-reference of geography × age × gender based on individual-level records from Batch 3 (Sep 2023, n=212) and Batch 5 (Sep 2024, n=173) — the two batches with complete joined location, birthdate, and gender data available (n=385 total). Each student's address was classified into the five geographic regions used throughout this dashboard. Findings should be treated as directional hypotheses for marketing experimentation. Batches 1, 2, 4, 6, 7, 8 were not cross-tabbed at individual level.

Significant findings
Eastern Estonia · n=39
Consistently the youngest cohort, by a meaningful margin
Avg age 25.3y vs 27.1y overall (−1.8y). In B5, 50% of Eastern students were under 21. The 40+ bracket is zero across both batches — Eastern Estonia sends exclusively younger students, likely coming straight from school rather than career-changing.

Implication: Career-change and reskilling messaging is likely less resonant here. School and vocational institution partnerships in Ida-Viru could reinforce the pipeline.
International · n=11
Highest female share of any region — 27% vs 18% overall
The pattern holds across both batches. International students are +9pp more female than the overall average. They also cluster in the 25–29 bracket (45%), suggesting mid-career movers rather than recent graduates.

Implication: International outreach channels (LinkedIn, international tech communities) may be a more efficient route to gender diversity than domestic marketing.
